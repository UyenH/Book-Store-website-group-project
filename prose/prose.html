The White Box

A high-pitched beeping pounds into my head. My eyes slowly crack open as the beeping continues to ring into my left ear. Bright light sweeps over my eyes, and a white blur lies in front of my vision. Eventually the blur comes to focus, and a white wall forms in front of me. I look over my left shoulder and I a mechanical monolith right beside. The machine has a menagerie of switches and buttons all over it, and it has a large screen on its center. Thin cables extend the side of the metal block and their ends are attached to my left arm and chest with suction cup like a tentacle. The screen seems to be measuring my vitals: heart rate, breathing, blood pressure and several other measurements. I look at my body and see that I am dressed in a hospital gown. Then I hear a loud slam in my right ear, and on my right side a person in a white scrub walks into the room pushing a cart of medicine and syringes on top. Beside the door is a large black mirror that stretches to the end of the wall. In my reflection I see that a ring-shaped scar wraps around the upper half of my head, and my hair are just stubbles on my scalp. The door locks behind him as he walks in. "Where the hell am I?" The stranger ignores me. He wears a stoic persona on his coffee brown skin. I notice he has a hook nose and big red lips, and my vision comes back to my memory. "You were the one standing over me while I dosed away to sleep." I snapped, "What are you some kind of ghost nurse?" He still answers me with silence, and the only sound comes from the beeping machine. I try to get off the bed, but my arms and legs just shake in place. I scan my body, and I see that I�ve been restrained. A transparent ring attached to the bed wraps around my biceps and fore arms of both my arms. The same rings wrap around my ankles, and a larger set of rings restrain my thighs. I keep struggling as my veins pop out of my muscles. "You crazy douchebag! You have no right to have me locked up here!" "We have every right to have you here," A disembodied voice echoes in the white box of a room. The voice is mechanical with a trace of static at the end of each spoken syllable. "You think scrambling your voice makes you look tough? Why don�t you crawl out of your black mirror and face me like a man," I reply. "Do not strain yourself. We barely managed to save you after your failed experiment." My mouth gapes open in shock. "How do you know about my machine?" I say calmly. "We will be the ones asking the questions," the voice replies. The nurse picks up a syringe from the cart filled with a scarlet solution. He grasps my left arm and injects the syringe into a vein on my wrist. I feel a slight sting which travels up my arm and into my head. "What did you put into me?" "We have some questions about your experiment from last night. Not everyone can construct a machine capable of breaching the flow of time. You are well aware that experimental time travel is a severe crime in our nation. How did you manage to get all the parts necessary for this machine?" My face remains quiet as the head of s statue. A moment of silence past, and the voice says, "Refusing to speak I see?" As soon as the question was spoken, I suddenly feel an intense burning in my body and gas starts to fume from my pores. "I was a researcher for Quantico. I led a team of scientist that experimented with photon energy.
