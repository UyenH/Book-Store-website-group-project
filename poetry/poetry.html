The Sandman 
                Hush dear child, don't 
                Be scared. I can sense the 
                clawing your back, digging its way 
                into your spine. I came to give you
                the consuming abyss of unconsciousness.
                Grasp my nocturnal robes and let the 
                Shadowy serpents coil around you. 
                My eyes are indiscriminate. 
                They are void of colors doubt and
                Mercy. They drain you're the dread
                from your eyes like a blackhole of
                infinite emotion.
Chew, Spit, Pass 
                I stroll down the forest of
                urban monoliths and spires that shadow
                the grime within its core. I notice
                a man patrolling with a vigilant
                intent in his gaze almost hungry as if he was a worn-out
                plug looking for an outlet to join with.
                He takes out a strip of mint gum wrapped in thin foil.
                I see a golden-haired peach breasted woman that is plump and ready for picking
                his gaze morphed into a mechanical desire that programmed
                him to latch onto the hunger. His balls pumping with the fuel of the
                grime of his lust.
                I hear the saliva ooze as his teeth
                Grind and chomp onto the
                sugar coated rubber and
                he sucks all the sweetness with a thick
                folding tongue.
                After he chews, chews, chews
                The gum spits from the infected mouth onto
                the sidewalks branded by a
                    myriad of ebony
                gum spots like a
                      Senile dalmatian waiting to be put
                         Down.

                The gum ages on and
                Hardens into an irremovable blemish that can never be
                cleaned.
                I pass on with a blinded gaze as a step onto the forgotten black spots
Yolanda 
                I prepare the table. Laying down
                the plates and utensils while Nanay
                finishes frying the bangus and the
                talong. As I listen to the sound of sizzling

                oil dying way, the crackling of rain
                rings on the roof. I brush of the sound.
                "Just another rain storm," I thought.
                But suddenly I hear the rain drops turn
                to bullets that bombarded our home. Then
                Tatay shrieks one word, "Bagyo"

                The water pierced through the concrete walls
                and the ceiling began to shed tears of preemptive
                mourning. My feet get buried by the sloshing
                flood and it swallows away chinelas with its
                abyssal mouth.  The dinning table and chairs began
                to float. Crashing into the walls and spilling our meal
                into the raging water and the lifeless bangus floats on its side.

                The wind blew with wrath
                Clogging our ears with clouds of cotton
                drowning out the screams from the outside
                of people being swept where they stand from outside.
                Like ants being blown by sadistic child.
                Then the wind's hands shook our house
                As if we were a present being ripped open.

                Finally, the battle subsided
                I see the result of the child's rage
                With all the blocks scattered across ground
                And all the lifeless dolls laying down
                But this peace is unsettling
                For what can we mere people do
                To win a war not against man
                But rather a daughter born from the atmosphere
